DESCRIPTION
  • Arsenic occurs in many minerals, usually in combination with sulfur and metals, but also as a pure elemental crystal.
  • It has various allotropes, but only the gray form, which has a metallic appearance, is important to industry.
  • Arsenic is a common n-type dopant in semiconductor electronic devices, and the optoelectronic compound
    gallium arsenide is the second most commonly used semiconductor after doped silicon.
  • Arsenic and its compounds, especially the trioxide, are used in the production of pesticides,
    treated wood products, herbicides, and insecticides.
  • A few species of bacteria are able to use arsenic compounds as respiratory metabolites.
