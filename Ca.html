DESCRIPTION
  • Calcium as the element is a grey silvery metal,the metal is rather hard.
  • Calcium is an essential constituent of leaves, bones, teeth, and shells.
  • Calcium is the fifth most abundant element in the earth's crust and
    makes up more than 3% of the crust.
  • Calcium does not occur as the metal itself in nature and instead is found in
    various minerals including as limestone, gypsum and fluorite.
  • Calcium is classified chemically as one of the alkaline earth elements,
    the metal is rather reactive.
  • It readily forms a white coating of calcium nitride (Ca3N2) in air.
