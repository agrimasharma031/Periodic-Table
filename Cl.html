DESCRIPTION
  • Chlorine is a greenish yellow gas which combines directly
    with nearly all elements.
  • Chlorine is a respiratory irritant.
  • The gas irritates the mucous membranes and the liquid burns the skin.
  • As little as 3.5 ppm can be detected as an odour, and 1000 ppm is likely
    to be fatal after a few deep breaths.
  • It was used as a war gas in 1915. It is not found in a free state in nature,
    but is found commonly as NaCl (solid or seawater).
