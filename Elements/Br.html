DESCRIPTION
  • It is the third-lightest halogen, and is a fuming red-brown liquid at room temperature that evaporates
    readily to form a similarly coloured gas.
  • Its properties are thus intermediate between those of chlorine and iodine.
  • Elemental bromine is very reactive and thus does not occur free in nature.
  • At high temperatures, organobromine compounds readily dissociate to yield free bromine atoms,
    a process that stops free radical chemical chain reactions.
  • Large amounts of bromide salts are toxic from the action of soluble bromide ion, causing bromism.
