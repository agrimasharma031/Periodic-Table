NAME Curium
SYMBOL Cm
ATOMIC NUMBER 96
APPEARANCE silvery metallic, glows purple in the dark
CLASSIFICATION Actinide
BOILING POINT 3383 K
MELTING POINT 1613 K
DESCRIPTION
  • Curium is a hard, dense, silvery metal with a relatively high melting point and boiling point for an actinide.
  • In compounds, curium usually exhibits valence +3 and sometimes +4, and the +3 valence is predominant in solutions.
  • When introduced into the human body, curium accumulates in the bones, lungs and liver, where it promotes cancer.
