DESCRIPTION
  • In its metallic state, iron is rare in the Earth's crust, limited to deposition by meteorites.
  • At least four allotropes of iron (differing atom arrangements in the solid) are known,
    conventionally denoted α, γ, δ, and ε.
  • The body of an adult human contains about 4 grams (0.005% body weight) of iron,
    mostly in hemoglobin and myoglobin.
  • Iron is also the metal at the active site of many important redox enzymes dealing
    with cellular respiration and oxidation and reduction in plants and animals.
  • Metallic or native iron is rarely found on the surface of the Earth because it tends to oxidize.
