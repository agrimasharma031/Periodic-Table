NAME Iodine
SYMBOL I
ATOMIC NUMBER 53
APPEARANCE Lustrous metallic gray, violet as a gas
CLASSIFICATION Reactive Non Metal
BOILING POINT 457.4 K
MELTING POINT 386.85 K
DESCRIPTION
  • The dominant producers of iodine today are Chile and Japan.
  • Iodine and its compounds are primarily used in nutrition.
  • It has high atomic number and is in ease of attachment to organic compounds.
  • It has found favour as a non-toxic radiocontrast material.
