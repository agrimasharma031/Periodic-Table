NAME Neodymium
SYMBOL Nd
ATOMIC NUMBER 60
APPEARANCE Silvery white
CLASSIFICATION Lanthanide
BOILING POINT 3347 K
MELTING POINT 1297 K
DESCRIPTION
  • It commonly exists in two allotropic forms.
  • It is paramagnetic at room temperature.
  • It becomes an antiferromagnet upon cooling to 20 K (−253.2 °C).
  • In order to make the neodymium magnets it is alloyed with iron, which is a ferromagnet.
