DESCRIPTION
  • Nickel belongs to the transition metals and is hard and ductile.
  • Pure nickel, powdered to maximize the reactive surface area, shows a significant chemical activity,
    but larger pieces are slow to react with air under standard conditions because an oxide layer
    forms on the surface and prevents further corrosion.
  • Native nickel is found in Earth's crust only in tiny amounts, usually in ultramafic rocks.
  • CNickel is slowly oxidized by air at room temperature and is considered corrosion-resistant.
