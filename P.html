NAME Phosphorous
SYMBOL P
ATOMIC NUMBER 15
APPEARANCE Colourless, waxy white, yellow, scarlet, red, violet, black
CLASSIFICATION Reactive nonmetal
BOILING POINT 553.7 K ​
MELTING POINT 317.3 K ​
DESCRIPTION
  • Phosphorus is commonly misspelled "phosphorous".
  • It is an essential component of living systems and is found
    in nervous tissue, bones and cell protoplasm.
  • Phosphorus exists in several allotropic forms including white (or yellow),
    red, and black (or violet).
  • White phosphorus has two modifications. Ordinary phosphorus is a waxy
    white solid. When pure, it is colourless and transparent.
  • It is insoluble in water, but soluble in carbon disulphide.
  • It catches fire spontaneously in air, burning to P4O10, often
    misnamed as phosphorus pentoxide.
