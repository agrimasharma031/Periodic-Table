NAME Selenium
SYMBOL Se
ATOMIC NUMBER 34
APPEARANCE Black, red, and gray
CLASSIFICATION Reactive nonmetal
BOILING POINT 958 K ​
MELTING POINT 494 K ​
DESCRIPTION
  • It rarely occurs in its elemental state or as pure ore compounds in the Earth's crust.
  • Selenium is found in metal sulfide ores, where it partially replaces the sulfur.
  • Minerals that are pure selenide or selenate compounds are known but rare.
  • ASelenium is a semiconductor and is used in photocells.
  • Selenium salts are toxic in large amounts, but trace amounts are necessary
    for cellular function in many organisms, including all animals.
