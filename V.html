DESCRIPTION
  • The elemental metal is rarely found in nature, but once isolated artificially, the formation of an oxide layer (passivation)
    stabilizes the free metal somewhat against further oxidation.
  • Vanadium occurs naturally in about 65 minerals and in fossil fuel deposits.
    which are widely distributed in the Earth's crust and lithosphere.
  • It is produced in China and Russia from steel smelter slag.
  • Other countries produce it either from magnetite directly, flue dust of heavy oil, or as a byproduct of uranium mining.
  • The vanadium redox battery for energy storage may be an important application in the future.
