DESCRIPTION
  • Carbon is a Group 14 element and is distributed very widely in nature.
  • It is a component of rocks as carbonates of calcium (limestone), magnesium, and iron.
  • The atmosphere of Mars is very thin but what there is contains about 95 % CO2.
  • Carbon is unique among the elements in the vast number of variety of compounds it can form.
  • Organic chemistry, a 1/112th subset of inorganic chemistry, is the study of carbon and its compounds.
  • While silicon might take the place of carbon in forming a host of related compounds, it is not
    possible currently to form stable compounds with very long chains of silicon atoms.
  • Carbon is found free in nature in three allotropic forms: amorphous, graphite, and diamond.
  • Graphite is one of the softest known materials while diamond is one of the hardest.
