NAME Californium
SYMBOL Cf
ATOMIC NUMBER 98
APPEARANCE silvery
CLASSIFICATION Actinide
BOILING POINT 1743 K
MELTING POINT 1173 K
DESCRIPTION
  • The element was named after the university and the state of California.
  • Two crystalline forms exist for californium under normal pressure: one above and one below 900 °C (1,650 °F).
  • Californium slowly tarnishes in air at room temperature. Compounds of californium are dominated by the +3 oxidation state
