NAME Fermium
SYMBOL Fm
ATOMIC NUMBER 100
APPEARANCE
CLASSIFICATION Actinide
BOILING POINT K
MELTING POINT 1800 K
DESCRIPTION
  • It is an actinide and the heaviest element that can be formed by neutron bombardment
    of lighter elements, and hence the last element that can be prepared in macroscopic
    quantities, although pure fermium metal has not yet been prepared.
  • A total of 19 isotopes are known, with 257Fm being the longest-lived
    with a half-life of 100.5 days.
  • Its chemistry is typical for the late actinides, with a preponderance of the +3
    oxidation state but also an accessible +2 oxidation state.
